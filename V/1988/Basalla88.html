- a primarily historical study, not an exercise in the philosophy or sociology of technology
Three main themes:
- diversity, an acknowledgement of the vast number of different kinds of artifacts, or made things, that have long been available
- necessity, the belief that humans are driven to invent artifacts to meet basic biological needs
- technological evolution, an organic analogy that explains both the appearance and the selection of these novel artifacts.

A close examination of these themes reveals that diversity is a fact of material culture, necessity is a popular but erroneous explanation of diversity, and technological evolution is a way of accounting for diversity without recourse to the idea of biological necessity.

- chapter 2 establishes artifact is the fundamental unit for the study of technology, and that continuity prevails throughout the made world

The existence of continuity implies that novel artifacts can only arise from antecedent
viii
artifacts - that new kinds of made things are never pure creations of theory, ingenuity, or fancy.

Chapters 3 and 4 survey the varied sources of novelty:
- human imagination
- socioeconomic and cultural forces
- the diffusion of technology
- the advancement of science
The conclusion drawn from this survey is that any society, at any time, commands more potential for technological innovation than it can ever hope to exploit.

Selection:
Because only a small fraction of ... possibilities are sufficiently developed to become part of the material life of a people, selection must be made from among competing novel artifacts. Ultimately, the selection is made in accordance with the values and perceived needs of society and in harmony with its current understanding of "the good life."
- selection process detailed in chapters 5 and 6

Chapter 7 addresses the issue of technological progress and human betterment.
The traditional conception of progress is found to be internally flawed and incompatible with technological evolution. However, progress can be redefined so that it no longer conflicts with an evolutionary perspective.
