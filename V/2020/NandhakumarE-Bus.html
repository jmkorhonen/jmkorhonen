Nandhakumar, S; Seenivasan, S; A. Mohammed Saalih, A. Mohammed; Saifudheen, Muhammed (2020). Weight optimization and structural analysis of an electric bus chassis frame. Materials Today: Proceedings.

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.matpr.2020.07.404.
(http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214785320355140)

.\Nandhakumar2020.pdf

  • Examines the effects of changing the chassis frame of Sunwin SWB6121EV57 electric bus from A709M grade 345 W structural steel to aluminium 6061-T6 or 7075-T6.
    • Gross vehicle weight + payload 18 000 kg
    • 60 passengers
    • gross weight of passengers 65 kg = 3900 kg
    • total load 21 900 kg
  • Weight original 11 504 kg
    • 6061-T6 3957.9 kg (65.61 % reduction)
    • 7075-T6 4103.2 kg (64.33 % reduction)
  • 7075-T6 shows less stress and deformation, making it an optimum material.

Abstract: Over the past decade, major cities in the world have been witnessing an increasing trend in motorization with deteriorating air quality, and there have been calls to promote public transport as a way out of this gridlock. It is in this context that electric buses can play a positive role, as there are several benefits related to the shift from conventional diesel buses to electric buses in terms of reduction in pollution, noise, and fuel consumption. Also, the cost of maintenance is about 25% lower than the maintenance cost of a diesel bus, since the electric motor doesn’t need the same level of services the diesel engine needs. However, the disadvantage of the electric vehicle lies in the endurance of the vehicle battery. Heavier the bus more is the energy required to run it. Reducing the weight of the vehicle parts is an optimum solution to the problem. The chassis frame which serves the function of giving structural safety to a vehicle at the same time incorporating different subsystems like steering and transmission plays a significant role in the determination of the entire vehicle's weight which in turn affects the vehicle’s performance and hence opted for this research. Most of the industries employ steel for the frame which possesses good strength but it adds more weight to the bus thus reducing the operating efficiency. This paper focuses on optimizing the weight of an existing electric bus chassis frame by replacing its steel members with potential aluminum alloys to reduce the weight without compromising its safety and strength. To ensure a safer design structural analysis is performed on the designed chassis frame model using ANSYS 20.0 simulation software taking stress and deformation as the design constraints.
Keywords: Electric bus chassis frame; Weight reduction; Aluminum alloys; Structural analysis; ANSYS
