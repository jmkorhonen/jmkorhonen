Focus on cobalt



  • Quantifies opportunities and limitations of circular economy strategies for lithium ion batteries, with a focus on cobalt
  • Co supply prone to three major supply risks:
    • mined primarily as a by-product of nickel (43%) and copper (44% in 2015); new mines require more nickel and copper mines
    • highly centralized: Congo, Chinese ownership of refining and mining
    • substitution is difficult and will remain partial for the foreseeable future
  • Relevant circular economy strategies:
    • reducing Co
    • eliminating Co
    • promoting reuse market for EV batteries in less-demanding applications
    • closed-loop battery recycling system
      • obstacles exist but potential is promising and already has had an important impact on the battery material industry in countries where batteries are produced in scale (China, S. Korea)
  • Study assumes expected future EV adoption rates to reach new EU vehicle emission targets; models prospective Co demand
    • includes trend towards high-Ni, low-Co chemistries, which has been faster than expected
    • assumes specific energy will increase to 235 Wh/kg on the pack level (this goal stated by the US DOE to make EVs commercially viable)
    • estimates average BEV in 2030 will have 86 kWh batteries, up from 42 kWh in 2017; this accounts for the higher share of SUVs
    • However, average Co content expected to increase only from 6.4 kg in 2017 to 10.3 kg in 2030 due to shift in chemistry
    • On the basis of expected EV demand and battery trends, total Co demand for EU EVs could reach 26.2 kt in 2030 and 57.4 kt in 2050
      • equals 22% and 48% of global Co mine production in 2017
      • Estimates of total Co reserves of current mines and ongoing late-stage exploration projects have identified total Co reserves in Europe of 58 kt
    • future supply projections based on cost of primary extraction and mine capacity estimate the future global annual mine supply capacity to be between 225-235 kt by 2025, and 190 kt with upper bounds between 237 and 311 kt by 2030
      • The role of DRC (Congo) is crucial
    • ASSUMES SECONDARY CO SUPPLY FROM BATTERIES LIMITED TO 2017 VEHICLE COLLECTION AND CO RECOVERY RATES (96%)
      • Results in a secondary Co supply of 5.9 kt by 2040 and 17.4 kt by 2050
    • Battery production capacity in the EU by 2030 6.1 million EV batteries, enough to supply 40% of all vehicles registered in 2030 (including ICEs)
    • Strong recycling and recovery policies could mean that peak virgin Co demand is reached in 2035, 33 kt/a
