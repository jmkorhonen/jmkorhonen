Reviewed LCAs published from 2008 onwards

1st generation: crystalline silicon (c-Si) base structure

  • single-crystalline silicon (sc-Si), multi-crystalline silicon (mc-Si)
  • little prospect that their price will drop further beyond 150 USD/M² because bulk of cost is due to the amount of material required and processes used
  • processes are generally energy intensive


2nd generation: thin film PVs

  • includes low thickness solar cells manufactured by the deposition of semiconductor layers on solid and low-cost substrates of glass, plastic, or metal. The semiconductor layer can be made with a compound or different materials such as cadmium, indium, gallium, tellurium, and copper. These materials are less expensive to manufacture than pure silicon, but geographically concentrated only in some areas and, in some cases, are hard to mine.
  • cost between 30-110 USD/m²
  • Cheapest ones are amorphous silicon (a-Si), but they degrade in sunlight

  • offers significant efficiency improvement, cost approximately 40-150 USD/m² for most efficient devices
