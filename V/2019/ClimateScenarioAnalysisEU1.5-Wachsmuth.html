1.5LIFE differs from 1.5TECH by including circular economy approaches and lifestyle changes in addition to technological approaches. Nevertheless,
some GHG emissions remain in both scenarios, in particular CO2 emissions from international transport, energy-intensive industries as well as non-CO2 emissions from agriculture and waste. These emissions are compensated by both natural carbon sinks from land use and artificial sinks based on carbon capture and storage (CCS), with triple the
amount of negative emissions in 1.5TECH than in 1.5LIFE.

Building renovation rates

1.5TECH 1.6%/year
1.5LIFE 1.7%/year for residential, 1.5%/year for service sector buildings

These can be considered realistic targets, since the lifetime of building shell components must be considered as basis for a renovations rate
Typical lifetimes of building elements range from 20-50 years, which can translate to replacement rates between 2% and 5%; for an average renovation rate, 3% is considered the upper realistic limit ( 2016:EnergRenovationsBuildings-EC )
