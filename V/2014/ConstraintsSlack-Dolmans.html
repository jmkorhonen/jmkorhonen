\cite{Dolmans2014}

- studied three new venture firms using longitudinal process approach & qualitative methods
- focus on entrepreneur's perceptions of resource availability
- studied three Dutch firms, one of whom went bankrupt
- constraints ("resource positions") are transient and imaginatory in nature
- entrepreneurs perceive resource availability in relation to demand; no "fixed" measures likely to be satisfactory

- see Honig, Lampel. Drori 2011


518

- identified three types of constraints:

  1. financial: relative availability of cash or other financial means
  2. capacity: operational or production capacity
  3. capability: human resources or know-how

540

- find that entrepreneurs not only engage in imaginative (re)combinations of existing resources but also can be guided by imaginative (re)combinations of perceived resource slack and resource constraints
