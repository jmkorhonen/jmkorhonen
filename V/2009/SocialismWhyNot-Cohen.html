The principles realized on the camping trip

Two principles are realized on the camping trip, an egalitarian principle, and a principle of community. The community principle constrains the operation of the egalitarian principle by forbidding certain inequalities that the egalitarian principle permits.

  • egalitarian principle is one of radical equality of opportunity, so it is consistent with certain inequalities of outcome
  • there are in fact a number of potentially competing egalitarian principles with which the camping trip complies, because the circumstances of the simple trip do not force a choice among them


  • SOCIALIST EQUALITY OF OPPORTUNITY: the egalitarian principle that _justice_ endorses
  • promoting equality of opportunity may reduce opportunities of those who benefit from inequality of opportunity
  • therefore, promoting equality of opportunity is not only an equalizing, but also a redistributing policy
  • We can distinguish three forms of equality of opportunity and three corresponding obstacles to opportunity

  1. so-called bourgeois equality of opportunity: characterizes, at least in aspiration, the liberal age. "Bourgeois equality of opportunity removes socially constructed status restrictions, both formal and informal, on life changes" (e.g. serfs, skin color)
  2. Left-liberal equality of opportunity goes beyond; and removes those circumstances of birth and upbringing that constrain not by assigning an inferior _status_ to their victims, but by nevertheless causing them to labor and live under substantial disadvantages; e.g. head-start education of children from deprived backgrounds. When fully achieved, people's fates are determined by their native talent and their choices, and, therefore, not at all by their social backgrounds. This corrects for _social_ but not for native or _inborn_ disadvantage.
  3. Socialist equality of opportunity treats the inequality that arises out of native (inborn) differences as a further source of injustice, since they are equally unchosen.

17

When socialist equality of opportunity prevails, differences of outcome reflect nothing but difference of taste and choice, not differences in natural and social capacities and powers.

  • in socialist equality of opportunity, income differences reflect nothing but different individual preferences, including income/leisure preferences
  • under a system where each gets the same income per hour, but can choose how many hours she works, it is not an intelligible complaint that some people have more take-home pay than others
