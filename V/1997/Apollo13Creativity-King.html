With no sleep and a shut-down computer, hurtling between the moon and earth encapsulated by a small weightless chamber connected only by a thin radio beam to NASA, creative problem solving was done under the gun. Some of the best kinds happen under extreme conditions such as these - Apollo 13 helps to remind us of that.

305

What can be gleaned from examining this episode of the space race for application to business, research, relationships - the whole gamut of problems and problem solving? First, sudden and severe limitations can evoke the highest order of creativity - but only if they are not allowed to abort the mission. Second, the automatic reflex when situations go bad is to get out rather than to make them better or turn them around. Third, the knee-jerk answer to loss or failure is to minimize trauma through damage control rather than in creative thinking. This response can be seen every day in the downsizing conducted by business in order to save face at the bottom line.
