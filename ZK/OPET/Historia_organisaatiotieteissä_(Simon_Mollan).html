Simon Mollan, University of York


Research Design and Historical Research in Management and Organization Studies


- has done work with complexity scientists, designing experiments
- open to visitors
- Important publications: Maclean, Harvey, and Clegg 2015
- Rowlinson, Hassard and Decker 2014 legitimized historical research publication in MOS
- there seems to be no Eisenhardt in management history

The role of historical study
- as history
- as theoretically sensitive historical research for hisotirographical development (makes historiographical contribution while using and engaging with theory)
- as theoreteically sensitive historical research for theoretical development (makes a theoretical contribution)
- as theoretical work that draws on, reflects, or considers temporal or historical factors in its development of new MO theory
- as philosophical and historiographical work that seeks to develop understanding of the methodology and understanding

Research strategies
- archive-led MY THING
- historiography-led (identifying a gap in existing historiography and contributing to it
- problem-led (informed by contemporary issues or problematized by concerns in wider social scientific or humanities discourse) MY THING
- Theory-led

Broad argument
- The history of History is of a tendency toward idiographic research: unique, non-generalizable, temporal (things past)
- Social sciences (tend) to aspire to be nomothetic, in line with natural sciences, developing laws and theories that explain things "objectively" and for all time (atemporal)
- Does this explain the problem of legitimating historical work in MOS better than the lack of an Eisenhardt?

Incommensurable?
- Is "History" incommensurable with the generation of theory in MOS? No... but many mainstream historians, including business historians, would think it was however
- They would also not always recognize the "history-work" in MOS as being "history" at all
- A theory-war?

Concluding argument
- Understanding historical work requires methodological reflexivity (sure!), but it also requires careful research design - to know what is done and why
- Articulation of research design with the different archetypes helps indicate the contribution
- Write more history! The point I have made to Gable Durepos is the legitimation of HOS (MOS-historic turn), is not when history is accepted in MOS, but when MOS scholars are publishing in mainstream history journals...
