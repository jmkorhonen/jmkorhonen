Definitions of collapse

Created Friday 13 November 2020
@2020 @collapse @ZK


V:2017:MiddletonCollapse , s. 12-

Tainter88: romahdus on poliittinen prosessi; se on tapahtunut kun yhteiskunta on menettänyt nopeasti merkittävän osan olemassaollutta sosiopoliittista kompleksisuutta. “Nopeasti” ei tarkoita välitöntä vaan muutamia vuosikymmeniä.

Tainterille romahdus on erittäin selkeästi poliittinen prosessi, eikä se ole epäonnistuminen vaan adaptaatio.
Mutta ovatko Tainterin esimerkit, Länsi-Rooman imperiumi, klassinen Maya-sivilisaatio, ja Pueblointiaanien yhteiskunta, yhteismitallisia?

George Cowgill ja Norman Yoffee 1988: The Collapse of Ancient States and Civilizations: V:1988:Cowgill88
Meidän pitäisi erotella selkeästi valtio (state), yhteiskunta (society), ja sivilisaatio, ja käyttää sanaa sivilisaatio erityisesti kulttuurillisessa mielessä. Romahdus tapahtuu sivilisaatioiden sisällä; sivilisaatiot ovat “kulttuurillisia perinteitä, joihin valtio on upotettu (embedded).” Ne koostuvat “kirjallisuudesta, tavoista, kielistä.”

Monesti sivilisaatio ei romahda lainkaan: Rooman, Maya, ja pueblosivilisaatio selviytyivät, muuttuneena, tähän päivään saakka. Erityiset poliittiset regiimit romahtivat, sosiaaliset järjestelmät muuttuivat, ja uskonnolliset ja ideologiset järjestelmät muuntuivat tai ne hylättiin.


V:2005:Collap-Diamond :

By collapse, I mean a drastic decrease in human populationsize and/ or political/ economic/ social complexity, over a considerable area, for an extended time. The phenomenon of collapse is thus an extreme form of several milder types of decline, and it becomes arbitrary to decide how drastic the decline of a society must be before it qualifies to be labelled as a collapse. 40

  • For Tainter, political collapse could have repercussions like depopulation; for Diamond, depopulation IS collapse.

15

V:2010:CollapseAfter-Schwartz :

In the archaeological literature, collapse usually entails some or all of the following: the fragmentationof states into smaller political entities; the partial abandonment or complete desertion of urban centers, along with the loss or depletion of their centralizing functions; the breakdown of regional economic systems; and the failure of civilizational ideologies. 42

V:2012:Endfield12 :

A more recent definition from their 2012 paper is given by archaeologist Karl Butzerand environmental historian Georgina Endfield, who suggest that:

Societal collapse represents transformation at a large social or spatial scale, with long-term impact on combinations of interdependent variables:
(i) environmental change and resilience;
(ii) demography or settlement;
(iii) socioeconomic patterns;
(iv) political or societal structures; and (v) ideology or cultural memory. 43

there is some overlap with the previous definitions, this one is different again, since it defines collapse as large- scale transformation with a number of possible impacts on five interlinked areas.



Finally, let us include a definition from the sustainability literature, as given by Marianne Young and Rik Leemans:

V:2007:YoungHuman-EnvironmentSystems p. 450

Let us define collapse as any situation where the rate of change to a system:

  • has negative effects on human welfare, which, in the short term, are socially intolerable;
  • will result in a fundamental downsizing, a loss of coherence, and/ or significant restructuring of the constellation of arrangements that characterise the system; and
  • cannot be stopped or controlled via an incremental change in behavior, resource allocation, or institutional values.
