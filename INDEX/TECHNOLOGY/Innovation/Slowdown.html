Indefinite growth requires constant Q



One of the assumptions that must hold for indefinite economic growth to happen in endogenous growth theory is that difficulty of finding new ideas (Q) must not increase.

There are no obvious reasons to believe that this should necessarily be the case (Jones 2019, p. 879).

In fact, evidence suggests that R&D productivity is decreasing, as low-hanging fruits have been harvested (Bloom 2020; Gordon 2016; Cowen 2011; Jones 2009, 2010).
