Solar PV


Inverters and transformers contribute the most to the high copper use of PV systems. In addition, roof-based systems have high aluminum requirements. Availability of critical metals may affect the choice of specific thin-film technologies ( V:2011:GraedelEnergyMetals ) but is unlikely to hinder the penetration of PV in the foreseeable future, i.e. through 2050 ( V:2012:SolarTerawatt-Fthenakis ). Due to lack of data, we do not predict any future changes in the material efficiency of transformers and inverters, but future research should investigate possible material efficiency gains from technological advances and economies of scale. V:2015:LCAGlobalRE-Hertwich p 16)

Woodhouse et al V:2013:RareEarthSupplyTeInGa-Woodhouse show that current (2015) production rates of tellurium and indium will allow a maximum of 9 GW per year of CdTe module production and a maximum of 28 GW per year of CIGS (copper indium gallium selenide) production. Increases in module efficiency and decreases in semiconductor layer thickness in thin films by 2030 and 2050 would allow for larger possible annual production of CdTe and CIGS than estimated by previous assessments. (Hertwich et al 2015, supplementary material p 16)

Elshkaki and Graedel ( V:2013:MetalsElectricity-Elshkaki ) indicate that tellurium availability will limit the application of CdTe solar cells, indium availability the use of CIGS, and Ag availability the use of silicon-based PV technologies. (Hertwich et al 2015, supplementary material p 16)


Critical materials


Most studies such as V:2015:LCAGlobalRE-Hertwich focus on bulk materials rather than critical metals (see V:2013:MaterialsModernSociety-Graedel)

Neodymium

Used in permanent magnets.

Dysprosium

Used in permanent magnets. If permanent magnet production constrained by Dy supply, wind power can rely on traditional gear box drives, samarium-cobalt magnets, or emerging nano-structured neodymium iron boron permanent magnets ( V:2014:DysproFreeMagnets-Brown , V:2014:DysproStrategies-Seo) that require little or no dysprosium. (V:2012:RareEarthAvailability-Alonso, V:2014:DysproFreeMagnets-Brown, V:2014:DysproStrategies-Seo)
